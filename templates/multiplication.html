Multiples Tips and Tricks

1: Every number is a multiple of 1

2: Multiples of 2 are even numbers

3: A number is a multiple of 3 if the digits in the number add up to a multiple of 3. This can be done until you're sure the sum is a multiple of 3. You can keep adding the digits until the sum is a single digit and see if the sum is a 3, 6, or 9, which are multiples of 3. Good for bigger numbers!

4: A number is a multiple of 4 if the digit in the tens and ones place make a number that is a multiple of 4. In other words, if the number made of the last two digits is a multiple of 4. Or if the ends in 00.

5: The last digit, the digit in the ones place is a 5 or a 0. The number ends in a 5 or a 0.

6: A number is a multiple of 6 if the number is a multiple of 2 ADD and multiple of 3.

7: A number is a multiple of 7 if you take the last digit of the number and times it by 2, then take that and subtract it from the number without the last digit. If the answer is a 0 or 7, then number is a multiple of 7. If still unsure, you can keep going by repeating the steps with the next digit.

8: If the digits in the hundreds, tens, and ones place make up a number that is a multiple of 8, then the number is a multiple of 8. In other words, a number is mulitple of 8 if the number made up by the last three digits is a multiple of 8. Or the number ends in 000.

9: If the sum of the digits is a multiple of 9. Can keep going until the sum is a single digit and see if it is a 9.

10: The last digit, the digit in the ones place is a 0. The number ends in a 0.

11: Add every other digit starting at the first number. Add every other digit starting at the second number. If the difference between the two numbers is 0 or 11, the number is a multiple of 11.

12: Multiples of 12 are multiples of 3 AND multiples of 4
